# Hungary 2026 Parliamentary Election — Reference

Factual reference on the Hungarian parliamentary electoral system and the 2026 election cycle.

---

## Election overview

- **Date:** 2026 April 12 (Sunday)
- **Body:** National Assembly (Országgyűlés)
- **Total seats:** 199
- **System:** Mixed-member electoral system
  - 106 seats via single-member constituencies (OEVK — Egyéni Választókerület), first-past-the-post, one round
  - 93 seats via national party lists, proportional allocation (d'Hondt method)

---

## Voter eligibility

- Hungarian citizens aged 18 or over on election day are eligible to vote.
- Citizens with a registered domicile in Hungary vote in person at their local polling station.
- Citizens without a Hungarian domicile (living abroad) may register for **postal voting** (levélszavazás) and cast their party-list vote by mail; they do not vote in a single-member constituency.
- Hungarian citizens who are abroad on election day but have a domestic domicile can vote at Hungarian diplomatic missions.

---

## Vote types

| Voter type | Vote 1 | Vote 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Domicile in Hungary | Constituency candidate (OEVK) | Party list or nationality list |
| No domestic domicile | — | Party list or nationality list (postal) |

Each eligible voter with domestic domicile therefore casts **two votes**: one for a local candidate, one for a national list. Voters without domestic domicile cast **one vote** (list only).

---

## Single-member constituencies (OEVK)

- 106 single-member constituencies in total.
- Distributed across Hungary's 19 counties and Budapest:
  - Budapest has 18 constituencies.
  - Each county has a number of constituencies proportional to its population (ranging from 2 to 10+).
- **Winner determination:** Simple majority (first-past-the-post). The candidate with the most votes wins, with no runoff round. A single round is held.
- Constituency boundaries were last redrawn in 2011 and remain in force for the 2026 election.

---

## National party lists and thresholds

A party may field a national list only if it has nominated candidates in at least **9 counties and Budapest**, covering at least **27 single-member constituencies** in total.

**Electoral threshold** (minimum share of valid party-list votes to receive any national list seats):

| List type | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Single-party list | 5% |
| Joint list of two parties | 10% |
| Joint list of three or more parties | 15% |

Parties and alliances that fail to reach their applicable threshold receive no national list seats.

---

## Nationality (minority) lists

- Hungary recognises 13 national minorities, each of which may form a nationality list.
- Nationality list formation requires recommendations from at least 1% of registered minority voters, with a cap of 1,500 recommendations.
- Nationality lists operate under a preferential seat quota: a list can win a seat with significantly fewer votes than a standard party list requires (roughly 1/4 of a regular mandate's quota).
- If a nationality list does not obtain a full seat, it may still elect a **nationality spokesperson** (nemzetiségi szószóló) with limited parliamentary rights.
- The combined vote share of all nationality lists has historically been small (approximately 0.44% in 2022).

---

## Seat allocation — national lists (d'Hondt)

After all 106 OEVK winners are determined, the national list seats (93) are allocated as follows:

1. **Fractional votes** are collected and added to each party's national list vote total:
   - Votes for all losing constituency candidates of a party.
   - Surplus votes for winning candidates (votes above the minimum needed to win their seat).
2. The combined total (direct list votes + fractional votes) forms each party's effective national list vote count.
3. Seats are allocated by the **d'Hondt method**: divide each party's total by 1, 2, 3, … and rank all resulting quotients; the top 93 quotients win seats.
4. Nationality lists receive their preferential allocation before the d'Hondt division is applied to regular party lists.

---

## Nomination rules

| Requirement | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Constituency candidate nomination (recommendations) | ≥ 500 |
| Party list eligibility (counties + Budapest covered) | ≥ 9 counties + Budapest |
| Party list eligibility (OEVK nominations required) | ≥ 27 single-member constituencies |
| Nationality list (% of minority voters, max 1,500) | ≥ 1% of registered minority voters |

---

## Campaign period and rules

- The official campaign period begins 50 days before election day and ends at midnight before the election.
- **Campaign silence (kampánycsend):** No campaigning is permitted from midnight on the day before the election until polls close.
- Public broadcast time is allocated equally among parties fielding national lists during the campaign period.
- Paid political advertising in public media is prohibited; commercial media rules apply separately.
- Outdoor advertising (billboard) rules limit campaign materials to designated surfaces during the campaign period.

---

## Election day procedure

- Polls are open from **06:00 to 19:00** local time (CET+1 / CEST).
- Voters present a valid ID at their assigned polling station; each is checked against the local register.
- Postal ballots must be received by the National Election Office by 19:00 on election day to be counted.
- Party and civil observer representatives may be present at all polling stations and counting locations.

---

## Result announcement and certification

- **Preliminary results** are announced progressively on election night as constituency results are reported.
- The National Election Office (Nemzeti Választási Iroda, NVI) publishes running tallies on [valasztas.hu](https://www.valasztas.hu) throughout the night.
- The **National Election Commission (Nemzeti Választási Bizottság, NVB)** is the official body responsible for certifying the final result.
- Official certification (the NVB's formal resolution) typically occurs within days to weeks after election day, once all disputes and postal ballots are resolved.
- Results are considered **final and frozen** only after NVB certification.

---

## Key procedural regulation

Electoral procedural deadlines for the 2026 parliamentary election are defined by **1/2026. (I. 13.) IM rendelet**, covering:
- Observer accreditation and committee timing
- Voter register and list deadlines
- Campaign and voting process deadlines
- Result establishment deadlines
